Mental fatigue and decision making

Introduction / Background

  1. Mental fatigue can impair core executive functions

  2. Decision making as executive function. Important aspect of decision making is risk taking.

  3. Does mental fatigue impair risk taking?

  4. Initial evidence that it does,

  5. But there is no standard protocol for inducing a transient state of mental fatigue, and (TO DO: check if true) individual working memory capacity not accounted

  6. This study will uses

  1. recently developed protocol to induce two types of mental fatigue based on small but impactful tweaks of the same activity (Stroop task)
  2. BART to evaluate risk taking
  3. well established complex span tasks to measure working memory capacity, as ability to maintain information in spite of interfering processing
  4. self report measures throughout of mental fatigue and associated concepts (sleepiness, boredom, workload)

  1. Previous evidence that computerized (and individualized) fatiguing protocols are effective and induce comparable self-report mental fatigue. Interestingly, despite the similar emntal fatigue, they achieve mental fatigue through different paths (overload, underload)

  2. Objectives:

  1. further evaluation of the fatiguing protocol
  2. does mental fatigue influence risk taking?
  3. does working memory moderate this influence?

Method

Participants 36 sport science students (18+ years old)

Design: Experimental within-subject study. All participants completed risk taking evaluation at baseline (i.e., no fatigue) and then after computerized activities designed to induce either underload fatigue and overload fatigue. Participation involved two days, one for the underload fatigue, and the other for the overload fatigue, in counterbalanced order. Baseline evaluation occurred either on the first or second day, counterbalanced, whereas on the other day, we evaluated participants’ pre-fatigue working memory capacity.

Objectives/hypotheses, analyses, results #1

Data-driven hypotheses:

  • 1.1 Our two manipulations successfully induced increased mental fatigue over time
  • 1.2 Sleepiness and …
  • 1.3 …boredom would also increase over time, but more for the underload than overload

Exploratory hypotheses:

  • The previous results would be moderated by working memory capacacity, whereby
    • 1.1b the increase in mental fatigue would be steeper for those with smaller working memory capacity, at least for the overload condition
    • 1.2 b
    • 1.3 b

Analyses:

  • linear mixed models (R, lmer, lmerTest)
  • categorical variable contrasts:
    • condition: a two-level variable (0=underload, 1=overload)
    • time: a four level variable (post25, post50, post75, post100) with polynomial contrasts for linear, quadratic, and cubic trends
  • working memory capacity mean centered
